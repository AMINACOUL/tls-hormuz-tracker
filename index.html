Situation Report

Brent fell to ~$98.83/bbl and WTI to ~$90.67 on March 24 — retreating from the $115+ peak following Trump's postponement of further Iran strikes, but the shock remains entrenched. Timor-Leste pump prices rose on March 9: Petrol RON91 $1.10→$1.23 (+11.8%); Diesel $1.05→$1.22 officially, but market diesel reached $1.40/L at Dili stations — $0.18 above the ANPM ceiling — triggering a Tane Konsumidór consumer protection alert (Mar 15). PM Xanana Gusmão committed to year-end supply security on March 19 and signalled fuel prices “not expected to increase.” Pertamina next cargo arrives Mar 25–27. No subsidy introduced — ESI fiscal discipline intact. Petroleum Fund at $18.6bn with depletion projected ~2037.

Key Watchpoints

Critical decisions ahead of April 15–17 TLER dissemination

1 — Subsidy Decision
Will PM’s Mar 19 signal become a formal ANPM ceiling directive?
If yes: ESI fiscal cost implications. If no: market diesel stays $0.18 above official, eroding ANPM credibility.
2 — New Cargo Price (Mar 25–27)
Will Pertamina’s incoming cargo be more expensive, triggering a second official hike?
Watch for ANPM announcement in early April if wholesale price has risen further.
3 — Brent Trajectory
Will retreat below $100/bbl hold? Trump postponement of strikes is the key variable.
Current: $98.83. Sustained easing could remove pressure for a second TL price round.
4 — ANPM Enforcement
Will ANPM enforce ceiling or revise it? Tane Konsumidór monitoring active.
Market diesel ($1.40) is 15% above official ceiling ($1.22). Enforcement action or ceiling revision both affect tracker data.
